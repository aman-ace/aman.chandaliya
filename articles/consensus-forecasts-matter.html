Why Consensus Forecasts Matter More Than the Number

By Aman Chandaliya · May 2026

Most public attention to revenue forecasts focuses on the headline number — the dollar figure that anchors the next year's budget. But the more interesting story is in how the number was produced. States with statutorily codified consensus processes tend to surface assumptions, disagreements, and uncertainty in ways that informal processes do not. That procedural transparency is, in many respects, more durable than any single forecast's accuracy.
