The Fiscal–Monetary Mix and Asset Prices

By Aman Chandaliya · March 2026

Abstract

This brief argues that the post-2020 era is defined less by central bank policy alone than by the interaction between fiscal and monetary stances — with significant implications for inflation and asset prices.

Setup

For most of the post-1980 era, monetary policy was the dominant macro lever. That balance has shifted: fiscal deficits and structural spending now meaningfully shape the cycle.

Framework

The brief offers a simple 2×2 framework — tight/loose fiscal × tight/loose monetary — and maps historical and current periods onto it.

Asset implications

Each quadrant has distinct implications for real rates, inflation breakevens, equity multiples, and the dollar.

Bottom line

Investors who watch only the central bank are likely to miss the most important macro variable of the next decade.
