Resilience Funding for Aging Public Infrastructure

By Aman Chandaliya · April 2026

Summary

This brief examines how state capital budgets account for climate-driven and structural risk, and proposes a resilience-linked appropriation framework that pairs maintenance obligations with statutory disclosure requirements.

Background

Aging infrastructure assets face a compound risk profile: physical deterioration, escalating climate exposure, and underfunded maintenance reserves. State budgeting practices vary widely in how — and whether — these risks are reflected in the capital budget.

Recommendations

1. Codify resilience risk disclosure in state statutory budget reports. 2. Tie a portion of capital appropriations to a formal long-horizon risk register. 3. Require periodic third-party resilience assessments for high-value assets.

Conclusion

Resilience funding is most effective when embedded directly in the statutory budget process rather than treated as a separate, optional layer.
