Working Paper

Estimating the Equity Risk Premium After 2020

By Aman Chandaliya · May 2026

Abstract

This working paper revisits common approaches to estimating the equity risk premium and asks how they should be adjusted for a post-2020 macro environment.

Introduction

The equity risk premium (ERP) is the most important — and most disputed — single number in finance. After a decade of low rates and a sharp regime shift in 2022, the standard estimation methods deserve a fresh look.

Methodology

I review three families of ERP estimates: historical averages, implied (forward-looking) ERPs derived from index-level cash flows, and survey-based estimates. Data is sourced from public market datasets, central bank releases, and academic databases.

Findings

Implied ERPs have re-rated meaningfully higher since 2022 as real yields rose, but historical-average estimates lag this shift. The gap argues for placing greater weight on forward-looking models in current valuations.

Discussion

Practitioners should be explicit about which ERP they use and why. The choice can swing fair-value estimates by 15–25%, often larger than the impact of growth assumptions.
