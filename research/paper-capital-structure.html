Working Paper

Capital Structure in a Higher-Rate World

By Aman Chandaliya · April 2026

Abstract

This paper examines corporate capital-structure choices in the U.S. as interest rates moved from sustained lows toward a higher steady state, and identifies the sectors most exposed.

Background

From 2010 to 2021, ultra-low rates encouraged aggressive debt issuance and shareholder returns funded by leverage. The new rate regime changes this calculus.

Approach

Using firm-level data on debt maturities, interest coverage, and credit ratings, I segment the U.S. corporate universe by refinancing pressure and sector cyclicality.

Findings

Refinancing walls cluster in 2026–2028 for several rate-sensitive sectors. Firms with low coverage ratios and near-term maturities face the steepest adjustments.

Implications

Investors should expect higher dispersion in returns across the credit spectrum and more selective M&A activity as cost of capital re-anchors.
