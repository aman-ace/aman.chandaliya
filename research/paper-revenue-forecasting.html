Working Paper

Consensus Revenue Forecasting Across U.S. States

By Aman Chandaliya · May 2026

Abstract

This working paper compares statutory consensus revenue forecasting in three U.S. states — Connecticut, Florida, and Wyoming — and evaluates how their procedural design influences forecast accuracy and budget transparency.

Introduction

Consensus revenue forecasting is a statutory mechanism by which executive and legislative branch staff jointly produce a single revenue estimate that anchors the state budget. While widely adopted, the design choices embedded in each state's framework vary considerably.

Methodology

This study draws on statutory text, official forecast publications, and historical revenue data spanning fiscal years 2010–2025. Sources were retrieved from state legislative archives, Web of Science, and Business Source Complete. Citations follow Chicago style.

Findings

Preliminary findings suggest that more transparent, statutorily codified consensus processes correlate with smaller absolute forecast errors, but that this relationship is moderated by economic volatility and the timing of the consensus meeting in the budget cycle.

Discussion

The results support continued investment in transparent statutory frameworks, while highlighting the need for better tools to communicate forecast uncertainty to legislators and the public.
