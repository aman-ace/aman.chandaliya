Literature Review

Budget Transparency and Statutory Reporting

By Aman Chandaliya · March 2026

Overview

This literature review surveys peer-reviewed scholarship on the relationship between state-level statutory reporting requirements and observed budget transparency outcomes.

Scope

Articles were sourced from Web of Science and Business Source Complete, focusing on U.S. state-level studies published between 2005 and 2025. The review centers on disclosure mandates, reporting timeliness, and citizen accessibility.

Key Themes

(1) Statutory mandates raise the floor of disclosure but do not guarantee usability. (2) Independent oversight bodies materially improve transparency outcomes. (3) Plain-language summaries are an under-studied lever for public engagement.

Gaps and Future Work

The literature lacks comparative empirical work on plain-language statutory summary requirements. This represents a promising direction for future research.
